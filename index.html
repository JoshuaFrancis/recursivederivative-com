Platform overview

Three engines that power institutional derivatives operations.

f(x)

Exotic pricing engine

A programmable payoff language allowing quants to define arbitrary derivative structures including barrier options, Asian options, variance swaps, and multi-asset rainbow products. Closed-form solutions are applied where available and Monte Carlo or PDE methods are selected automatically based on payoff complexity. Greeks are computed analytically or via automatic differentiation with configurable bump sizes and parallelised across GPU clusters.

∂²

Volatility surface calibration

Real-time calibration of local volatility, stochastic volatility, and rough volatility models to live options market data across expiry and strike dimensions. SVI, Heston, and SABR parameterisations are available with automatic arbitrage detection and surface smoothing. Calibrated surfaces are stored and versioned, enabling historical attribution of pricing differences to model recalibration versus underlying price movement.

∑n

Portfolio risk analytics

Aggregated Greeks, scenario P&L, VaR, CVaR, and stress test reporting across multi-asset, multi-model derivative portfolios in a single unified risk framework. Risk decomposition by underlying, model parameter, and payoff feature allows traders and risk managers to understand exactly where P&L sensitivity lies and where hedging costs can be reduced by adjusting exposure concentrations across the book.
